Part III

The Message I Never Sent

There is something I wrote one night. And I unsent it.

Not because it wasn't true, but because I wasn't brave enough to let you see that side of me.

About the day I opened up to you? I was reading my own texts and I didn't recognize my own words. I read them one by one and I cried.

I was like damn... this is so sad. Why can't my brain accept that it's me? That these were my own texts?

I have NEVER talked about this to anyone so clearly. But I guess opening up works better when you don't think, when you just open your heart and revisit the past.

I am starting to realize the one big problem I have is I do anything to protect myself. Even in front of a therapist.

I would twist my story in a way so I get what I want, so she knows as little as possible, so I can just get it over with. I admit I should have been completely honest. But I wasn't. I felt proud that I manipulated the situation. And that scared me.

What is wrong with me? Why do I manipulate? Why do I hurt people without realizing? What's the problem and I will fix it I swear. Just tell me what's wrong with me.

When I reread that message, I cried. Not because I am evil. But because I finally saw how much I had been protecting myself.

The therapist explanation is simple: When vulnerability feels dangerous, control feels safer.

It wasn't that I was a monster. It was that I was scared of being fully seen and rejected. I didn't send that message because I wasn't ready. But I am now.
